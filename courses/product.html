Course Introduction

This course is positioned as an introductory course for beginners in product design. The course starts from the job requirements of product designers, mainly learning the learning path and methods of product design, as well as the design process and skills that should be mastered in design practice. Through the study and practice of a large number of cases in this course, learners will establish the basic concepts, work skills, and methods of product design, and be able to apply the knowledge and skills learned to evaluate and practice product design, thereby improving their practical and innovative abilities.

Course Objectives

√ 1. Clearly articulate and understand the professional ethics requirements and development path of product designers.

√ 2. Use product design related software (2D drawing software) to create product design sketches and renderings.

√ 3. Apply product design related software (3D modeling, rendering software) for product design practice activities.

√ 4. Participate in classroom activities on time and actively, take on individual responsibilities within the team by completing classroom group tasks, utilize collaborative methods to analyze, evaluate, or complete the entire process of product design works.

Course Content

Semester 1
Week1 Unit 1: Methodology of Product Design Learning 1 Be able to explain the concept, content, and learning methods of product design, retell the main characteristics of the development history of industrial design in the world, compare the features of typical cases in Danish design, German design, and Japanese design, and analyze their impact on the design field.
Week2 Unit 2: Methodology of Product Design Learning 2 Be able to complete a design schedule based on the general process of product creative design expression, and able to use relevant software for layout design.
Week3 Unit 3: Hand drawing and image software 1 Be able to identify the hand drawn expression methods of product design creativity, remember the learning methods of product design software, and self-study a design software of interest using this method. Be able to identify the elements of excellent design and conduct analysis of excellent design drawings for product design.
Week4 Unit 4: Hand drawing and image software 2 Be able to recognize the interface partition of image software PS and use basic operations related to PS and hand drawing to draw a simple work.
Week5 Unit 5: Hand drawing and image software 3 Be able to follow the tutorial, use commonly used PS tools and commands, and create a comprehensive work.
